As a PhD student at the University of Twente, I am studying computer vision under the supervision of Raymond Veldhuis, Christoph Brune, and Nicola Strisciuglio. My research focus is on enhancing the robustness of deep learning-based computer vision systems against common image corruptions such as noise and blur. Meanwhile, I work on cell segmentation and classification with the Microscope images provided by BIOS lab at University of Twente. 
I have gained an in-depth understanding of the data processing of computer vision systems from a Fourier perspective. By analyzing the frequency components of the input data, I have discovered that many computer vision systems rely on frequency shortcuts to achieve their objectives quickly, which may impact their robustness. To address this issue, I am exploring efficient learning techniques that utilize data in a more effective manner during training.
